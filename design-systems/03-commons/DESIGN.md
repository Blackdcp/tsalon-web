# T Salon — Commons

> Direction 03 · A common table for people who build technology.

## 1. Position

T Salon is framed as a technically rigorous commons: a place where developers, researchers and makers meet as people. Warmth comes from paper, daylight, documentary photography and editorial pacing, not friendly startup illustrations.

### Principles

1. See people before scale.
2. Warmth comes from material and light.
3. Openness still needs strong information architecture.
4. The Logo remains a technical signature inside a human environment.

## 2. Logo

- Use the existing dark lock-up on Paper, Cream or white.
- Reverse to white on Ink only.
- Minimum width: 112 px; clear space equals one symbol stroke-width.
- Signal Orange and Sun never recolour the Logo.
- Avoid putting the Logo on highly detailed or orange-dominant photography.

## 3. Color

| Token | Value | Role |
|---|---:|---|
| Ink | `#20201E` | Primary text and dark section |
| Paper | `#FFFCF5` | Main canvas |
| Cream | `#FFF3D1` | Community/editorial field |
| Rule | `#E5D8B9` | Dividers and quiet boundaries |
| Body | `#68645B` | Secondary text |
| Signal | `#F4511E` | Primary action, link and key marker |
| Sun | `#FFC857` | Highlight field and small annotation |

Warm colours should behave like paper and ink. Avoid beige-on-beige low contrast and decorative orange gradients.

## 4. Typography

- Display Chinese: `Noto Serif SC`, 600/500.
- Interface and body: `Noto Sans SC`, 600/500/400.
- Technical metadata: `IBM Plex Mono`, 500/400.
- Hero: 64–84 px, line-height 1.08–1.14.
- Editorial heading: 34–52 px; body: 16–18 px, line-height 1.85.
- Serif carries propositions and narrative; sans carries facts, labels and actions.

## 5. Layout

- Maximum width: 1440 px; 12 columns.
- Desktop outer gutter: 40 px; mobile gutter: 20 px.
- Spacing scale: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 32, 40, 48, 64, 96, 120.
- Most editorial frames are square; controls may use a restrained 2–4 px radius.
- One offset edge or coloured paper layer can mark a major feature—never every card.

## 6. Components

### Actions

- Primary: Signal Orange with light text.
- Secondary: transparent with Ink outline.
- Inline link: Ink underline by default, Signal on hover/focus.

### Event card

Photography is allowed to take half or more of the card. Facts remain structured alongside it: date, city, format, registration state, capacity and access requirements.

### Community letter

A first-person or editorial format for organizational reflection. It has a named author/editor, publication date, short summary and links to the events or people it references.

### Topic tag

Topic labels use thin outlines and monospaced uppercase Latin when appropriate. Do not use warm colour to make every label look promotional.

## 7. Photography

- Preserve skin tones, venue colour and available light.
- Choose images showing exchange, attention and shared activity.
- Use generous crops; do not mosaic many tiny images.
- Warm colour belongs around the image, not as a universal overlay.

## 8. Motion

- 180–260 ms, standard ease-out.
- Physical metaphors are limited to paper-like 2–4 px shifts on hover.
- No bouncing cards or perpetual decorative movement.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

## 9. Homepage application

The first screen combines a large serif proposition, concise sans-serif explanation and one real community photograph. A next-event rail makes the organization feel active. Following sections alternate Paper, Cream and Ink to separate events, knowledge and institutional story.

## 10. Accessibility and search

- Check orange button and muted copy contrast at final font sizes.
- Keep content as selectable HTML; images support, never replace, meaning.
- Use permanent event archives, author/speaker pages, topic indexes and descriptive internal links.
- Add `Organization`, `Event`, `Article`, `Person`, `BreadcrumbList` and contextual `FAQPage` structured data.
- Every published page contains a clear summary, factual metadata and citation-friendly subheadings for SEO/GEO reuse.
